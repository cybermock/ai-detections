# AI-031 - AI-Generated Phishing Indicators

> **Detection ID:** AI-031
> **Status:** Active
> **Risk Score:** 40 (user) + 20 (system)
> **MITRE ATT&CK:** T1566.001 - Phishing: Spearphishing Attachment (Initial Access)
> **Data Model:** Web + Email
> **Schedule:** `*/5 * * * *` (every 5 minutes)
> **Lookback Window:** `-15m@m` to `now`
> **Kill Chain Phase:** Staging
> **Last Updated:** 2026-02-22

---

## Overview

This detection identifies a behavioral pattern strongly associated with AI-assisted phishing campaigns: a user interacts heavily with AI services and then sends a high volume of outbound emails within the same time window. AI tools like ChatGPT and Claude can rapidly generate convincing, grammatically polished phishing emails at scale, including personalized spearphishing messages that would traditionally require significant manual effort.

The threat this detection addresses is the weaponization of AI for social engineering. A compromised account, malicious insider, or negligent user can use AI to generate dozens of unique phishing email variants in minutes, each customized for a specific target. This represents a fundamental shift in the phishing threat landscape: AI dramatically reduces the cost and time required to produce high-quality phishing content while increasing its effectiveness.

The detection correlates two data sources: AI service web traffic (from the Web data model) and outbound email send events (from the Email data model). When a user generates 5+ AI requests AND sends 10+ emails within the same 1-hour window, the correlation fires. The risk scores are elevated (40 user + 20 system) because the combination of AI content generation and mass email delivery strongly suggests phishing preparation and execution. This detection is also relevant for insider threat scenarios where an employee uses AI to craft social engineering messages targeting coworkers.

## Detection Logic

### Plain English

1. Query the `Web` data model for AI service traffic within 1-hour windows, grouped by user. Enrich via `ai_domains_filter`.
2. Query the `Email` data model for outbound send events (`action=send`) within the same 1-hour windows, grouped by source user.
3. Join the two datasets by user, keeping only records where both AI requests and email sends exist.
4. Filter for users with 5+ AI requests AND 10+ emails sent in the same window.
5. Exclude sanctioned users/systems via `ai_unsanctioned_filter`.
6. Tag the event with `detection_id=AI-031`.

### Key Fields

| Field | Description | Example Value |
|---|---|---|
| `user` | User with AI + email correlation | `tsmith@corp.com` |
| `src` | Source endpoint | `10.1.2.55` |
| `ai_providers` | AI providers used | `OpenAI, Anthropic` |
| `ai_requests` | Number of AI service requests | `25` |
| `ai_bytes` | Total bytes uploaded to AI services | `524288` |
| `emails_sent` | Number of emails sent in window | `47` |

### Thresholds

| Parameter | Default Value | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Minimum AI requests | 5 | AI interaction count to qualify |
| Minimum emails sent | 10 | Outbound email count to qualify |
| Time window | 1 hour | AI requests and email sends must fall within same hour |

## Triage Steps

> **Estimated triage time:** 15-25 minutes

### Step 1: Validate the alert and note the volumes

```spl
index=risk search_name="AI RBA - AI-031: AI-Generated Phishing Indicators" risk_object="$user$" earliest=-24h
| table _time risk_object risk_score risk_message
| sort -_time
```

- Note the AI request count and email send count.
- Very high email counts (50+) with substantial AI interaction are high priority.

### Step 2: Identify the user and role

```spl
| inputlookup identity_lookup_expanded
| search identity="$user$"
| table identity first_name last_name email managedBy category priority bunit department
```

- Marketing, sales, and communications teams may legitimately send high email volumes.
- Users in technical roles sending mass emails is more suspicious.
- Check if the account may be compromised (recent password change, MFA anomalies).

### Step 3: Examine the AI interaction details

```spl
| tstats summariesonly=t count sum(Web.bytes_out) as bytes_out values(Web.url) as urls values(Web.http_user_agent) as user_agents
  from datamodel=Web where Web.user="$user$" earliest=-4h
| rename Web.* as *
| eval ai_domain=lower(url_domain)
| lookup ai_provider_domains domain as ai_domain OUTPUTNEW provider enabled
| where enabled=1
| stats sum(bytes_out) as total_bytes sum(count) as requests values(urls) as urls values(user_agents) as agents by provider
| eval mb_out=round(total_bytes/1024/1024,2)
```

- What AI providers were used?
- How much data was uploaded (inputs to AI) vs. the timeframe?
- Are URL patterns suggesting conversational interaction or API-based generation?

### Step 4: Examine the outbound email activity

```spl
| tstats summariesonly=t count values(Email.recipient) as recipients values(Email.subject) as subjects
  from datamodel=Email where Email.src_user="$user$" Email.action="send" earliest=-4h
| rename Email.* as *
| stats count as total_sent dc(recipients) as unique_recipients values(subjects) as subjects
```

- How many unique recipients? Mass phishing targets many distinct recipients.
- Are the email subjects similar (template-based) or unique (personalized)?
- Do the subjects contain urgent language, credential reset requests, or financial themes?

### Step 5: Check for phishing indicators in email content

```spl
| tstats summariesonly=t count values(Email.subject) as subjects values(Email.recipient) as recipients
  from datamodel=Email where Email.src_user="$user$" Email.action="send" earliest=-4h
  by Email.subject
| rename Email.* as *
| where match(lower(subject),"urgent|password|verify|confirm|account|suspended|invoice|payment|action required|security alert|click here|update your")
| stats count as suspicious_subjects values(subject) as subjects
```

- Subjects containing urgency keywords, credential-related language, or financial themes are strong phishing indicators.
- Multiple emails with similar templates but different recipients suggest a campaign.

### Step 6: Check for signs of account compromise

```spl
index=risk risk_object="$user$" earliest=-7d
| search NOT search_name="AI RBA*"
| stats sum(risk_score) as non_ai_risk values(search_name) as non_ai_detections by risk_object
```

- If non-AI security detections have fired for this user, the account may be compromised.
- Look for authentication anomalies, impossible travel, or credential stuffing indicators.

### Step 7: Correlate with other AI detections

```spl
index=risk risk_object="$user$" search_name="AI RBA*" earliest=-24h
| stats sum(risk_score) as total_risk values(search_name) as detections by risk_object
```

- AI-031 + AI-007 (high volume upload) = large amounts of content generated and sent.
- AI-031 + AI-015 (after-hours) = phishing campaign preparation outside business hours.
- AI-031 + AI-028 (unsanctioned access) = using unauthorized AI tools for email generation.

### Step 8: Make a determination

- **True Positive:** User (or compromised account) used AI to generate phishing email content and distributed it at scale.
- **Benign True Positive:** Marketing team member using approved AI tool to draft legitimate campaign emails.
- **False Positive:** User researching AI while coincidentally sending routine business emails.

---

## True Positive Indicators

- Email subjects contain phishing language (urgency, credential requests, financial themes).
- High unique recipient count suggests a targeted campaign rather than routine communications.
- AI requests show substantial content generation (high bytes_out).
- Email content has been flagged by email security tools for suspicious patterns.
- User's role does not justify mass email sending.
- Account shows signs of compromise (anomalous logins, MFA bypass).

---

## False Positive Scenarios

| Scenario | Likelihood | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| Marketing team using AI to draft legitimate email campaign copy | High | Allowlist marketing team members; validate with marketing lead |
| Sales team using AI for personalized outreach emails | High | Allowlist sales teams; adjust email threshold upward for sales roles |
| HR sending company-wide announcements while using AI for drafting | Medium | Allowlist HR broadcast accounts |
| User browsing AI service while email auto-responders fire | Low | Exclude auto-reply/OOO emails from the email count |
| Customer support using AI to generate response templates | Medium | Allowlist support teams with documented AI usage approval |

---

## Response Actions

### If confirmed True Positive

1. **Contain:** Immediately disable the user's email sending capability. If the account is compromised, force a password reset and revoke active sessions.
2. **Notify:** Alert the SOC lead, email security team, and the user's manager. If phishing emails were sent externally, notify affected recipients.
3. **Investigate:** Examine the full email recipient list and content. Determine if any recipients clicked links or opened attachments. Check if the AI service was used to generate malware, payloads, or credential harvesting pages.
4. **Remediate:** Recall or quarantine sent phishing emails. Block identified phishing URLs/domains. Deploy targeted user awareness training.
5. **Document:** Record the full scope of the phishing campaign including recipients, content themes, and AI provider used.

### If Benign True Positive

1. Add the user/team to `ai_sanctioned_entities.csv` with appropriate provider and email workflow documentation.
2. Adjust email thresholds for legitimate mass-email roles.
3. Close the notable as "Benign True Positive."

---

## Tuning Guidance

### Reducing false positives

- Increase the email threshold from 10 to 25 for environments where mass email is common.
- Exclude users in marketing, sales, and HR roles who regularly send high volumes of email.
- Add a minimum AI bytes_out threshold to ensure the AI interaction involves content generation, not just browsing.

### Measuring AI-to-email correlation rates

```spl
| tstats summariesonly=t count from datamodel=Web where Web.url_domain=* earliest=-7d latest=now by Web.user _time span=1h
| rename Web.* as *
| eval ai_domain=lower(url_domain)
| lookup ai_provider_domains domain as ai_domain OUTPUTNEW provider enabled
| where enabled=1
| stats sum(count) as ai_requests by user _time
| join type=left user _time [| tstats summariesonly=t count from datamodel=Email where Email.action="send" earliest=-7d latest=now by Email.src_user _time span=1h
  | rename Email.src_user as user, count as emails_sent]
| where ai_requests>=5 AND emails_sent>=10
| stats count as correlation_windows dc(user) as unique_users by user
| sort -correlation_windows
```

Review the top correlating users to identify legitimate patterns that should be allowlisted.
